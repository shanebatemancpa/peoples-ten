01 — The Betrayal

The promise was real.
The plan was not.

The promise was simple: put regular people first. What arrived instead was a government for sale — at every level, in every chamber, in every city hall and statehouse and federal agency. From the school board to the Senate, the pattern is the same. This was not incompetence. It was a transaction.

"The agencies are hollowed out. The institutions are captured. The rules of democracy are being rewritten — by the people who benefit from rewriting them."

Institutional Capture

From federal agencies to local school boards — public institutions handed to political loyalists. Oversight dismantled. Watchdogs fired. At every level, the people's institutions are being converted into instruments of private power.

Government for Sale

Multimillion-dollar properties orbiting the Oval Office. The seat of American government converted into a table where access is purchased, not earned — and the price of entry is a check to the right campaign.

Incompetence by Design

Donor-paid appointees placed atop agencies they were chosen to dismantle — flying on custom jets at taxpayer expense. The chaos is not accidental. It is the point.

Rigging the Rules

Legislatures across the country redrawing congressional maps mid-decade — not after a census, but after an election they want to protect. The rules are being changed by the people the rules are supposed to constrain.

03 — The Platform

Three pillars.
One country back.

Three principles that apply at every level of government — from city council to the Oval Office. Simple enough to fit on a bumper sticker. Powerful enough to rebuild a democracy.

01
Pillar One

Your Government, Not Theirs

Government belongs to the people it serves — at every level. From the county commissioner to the cabinet secretary, public office is a public trust. Not a private opportunity.

  • End pay-to-play appointments at every level of government.
  • Protect public institutions from political capture — local, state, and federal.
  • Prohibit personal financial enrichment through any public office.
  • Reinstate and strengthen independent oversight at every level.
"Public servants. Not private servants."
02
Pillar Two

Your Vote, Your Rules

The rules of democracy cannot be rewritten by the people who benefit from rewriting them — at any level. City councils, state legislatures, Congress: the same principle applies everywhere.

  • Ban on partisan redistricting at every level — federal, state, and local.
  • Automatic voter registration and standardized election access nationwide.
  • Term limits and lifetime lobbying bans for all elected officials.
"You pick your leaders. Leaders don't pick their voters."
03
Pillar Three

Your Tax Dollars, Your Return

Every dollar of public money — federal, state, or local — belongs to the people who paid it. Transparency is not optional. Accountability is not partisan. It is the basic contract of governance.

  • Full public accounting of spending at every level of government.
  • Zero public funds for personal branding or embellishment of any public property.
  • Mandatory independent audit after any leadership transition — from mayor to president.
"It's your money. Act like it."

I — Misconduct in Public Office Act
Thou shalt answer for every abuse and every neglect of the duty we gave thee.
Federal criminal liability for willful abuse or neglect of public duty — acts done AND duties deliberately left undone. "It wasn't technically illegal" is no longer a defense.

Thou shalt not stand between any citizen and their ballot.
Automatic voter registration. 15 days minimum early voting. Election Day as a federal holiday. Paper ballot backups required. The ballot is the people's weapon. No one gets to take it away.
III — The Fair Maps Act
Thou shalt not choose thy voters. They chose thee — and they can unchoose thee.
Federal ban on mid-decade partisan redistricting. Independent commissions required by law. The most dangerous sentence in American politics — and the most important law to pass.
IV — The Public Trust Restoration Act
Thou shalt not silence the watchmen we appointed to guard against thee.
Removing an Inspector General requires a two-thirds Senate supermajority. IGs gain independent prosecutorial referral authority — bypassing the Attorney General. The watchdogs answer to the people, not the person being watched.
V — The Sunshine Expenditure Act
Thou shalt account for every dollar spent in our name, in the light, in plain sight.
Real-time public disclosure of all executive travel and spending above $10,000. Live searchable federal dashboard. No custom-fitted aircraft or vehicles for political appointees. If you wouldn't spend your own money on it, don't spend ours.
VI — X
VI — The Grift Tax Act
Thou shalt not profit from the trust the people placed in thee.
75% punitive clawback on all income accrued through government access or preferential treatment. Clawback window: 10 years. Proceeds fund public oversight. Your enrichment is their loss — and the law will take it back.
VII — The Golden Rule Act
Thou shalt not gold-plate, rename, or brand what belongs to the people and not to thee.
Zero public funds for personal branding of federal property. Violators pay personally for full restoration. Congressional approval required for major renovations. The People's House is not a hotel. The Rose Garden is not a parking lot.
VIII — The Emoluments Enforcement Act
Thou shalt serve no sovereign, no interest, and no master but the people of this republic.
Mandatory blind trusts. Private right of action for emoluments violations. Dedicated GAO oversight panel with subpoena power. The Constitution already said this. Now it has teeth.
IX — The Appointment Integrity Act
Thou shalt not sell the people's government to those who funded thy ambition.
5-year ban on appointing major campaign donors. Senate confirmation required for all agency heads. No more buying a cabinet seat. The people's agencies belong to the people.
X — The Lobbying Cooling Off Act
Thou shalt not sell the seat we lent thee the moment thou hast vacated it.
Lifetime lobbying ban for all members of Congress. 10-year ban for all appointed officials. Post-Government Employment Registry — public and searchable. The people's seat is not a stepping stone to a billionaire's payroll.

05 — The Policy Ledger

Ten laws.
One governing mandate.

Each commandment is a named law with a mechanism, a penalty, and a political argument. Not a wish list — a legislative checklist candidates sign and voters track.

Pillar 1
I. Misconduct in Public Office Act
Federal criminal liability for willful abuse or neglect of public duty
＋

Definition of the Offense

A public officer commits an offense if they willfully neglect a legally obligated duty OR abuse their powers for personal, financial, or partisan gain — covering both acts of commission and omission.

Prohibited Conduct

  • Appointing grossly unqualified individuals with willful disregard for public harm.
  • Directing agency resources toward personal enrichment or donor benefit.
  • Deliberately dismantling or defunding oversight functions — IGs, ethics offices, watchdog agencies.
  • Using public office to coerce, threaten, or intimidate private citizens or institutions.
  • Willfully misrepresenting government activity to the public or Congress.
Penalty: Up to 7 years federal imprisonment + permanent bar from public office
Why it works: The US is one of the few democracies without this law. The UK has had it for centuries. It closes the "it wasn't technically illegal" loophole that has shielded misconduct for decades.
Pillar 2
II. The Democracy Access Act
Automatic registration and standardized federal election access for every American
＋
  • Automatic voter registration upon interaction with any federal or state agency. Opt-out, not opt-in.
  • Minimum federal standards: 15 days early voting, same-day registration, accessible polling within defined distance thresholds.
  • Federal criminal penalties for deliberate actions to reduce polling access or purge eligible voters.
  • Election Day declared a federal public holiday.
  • Nationwide paper ballot backup requirement for all federal elections.
Violation: $10M fine per cycle + federal electoral supervision for 10 years
Why it works: Making it easier to vote is popular with everyone except those who need low turnout to win. "Your vote, your right" rather than a partisan issue — this flips the script on those who oppose it.
Pillar 2
III. The Fair Maps Act
Federal ban on mid-decade partisan redistricting. Independent commissions required.
＋
  • No state may redraw congressional district boundaries more than once per decade, following the decennial census.
  • All states must establish independent redistricting commissions — members barred from partisan office for 10 years prior to appointment.
  • All redistricting requires public hearings and a 60-day public comment period.
  • Federal judicial fast-track for challenges — dedicated three-judge panel, mandatory 90-day resolution.
  • Any map drawn in violation is automatically void, replaced by the prior valid map.
Violation: State loses federal election funding equal to 3x the estimated partisan advantage gained
Why it works: "They're drawing the lines so you can't vote them out" is immediately understood by voters of every party. This is one of the clearest illustrations that the system is being rigged in real time.
Pillar 1
IV. The Public Trust Restoration Act
Reinstates and permanently protects all Inspectors General
＋
  • Immediately reinstates all IGs removed without statutory cause since January 20, 2025, with full back pay.
  • Removing any IG requires a two-thirds Senate supermajority — no unilateral presidential firing.
  • IGs gain independent prosecutorial referral authority — directly to a special counsel, bypassing the AG.
  • All IG reports transmitted to relevant congressional committees within 30 days regardless of executive instruction.
  • Creates the Office of Institutional Integrity — an independent agency reporting directly to Congress.
Violation: Unlawful removal of an IG = obstruction, felony, up to 5 years
Why it works: The mass firing of IGs was one of the most flagrant acts of oversight destruction in American history. This law names the act, reverses it, and ensures it can never happen unilaterally again.
Pillar 3
V. The Sunshine Expenditure Act
Real-time public disclosure of all executive travel, perks, and renovations
＋
  • Real-time public disclosure within 72 hours of all non-classified government travel and expenditures for political appointees and elected officials.
  • Mandatory live searchable federal expenditure dashboard — all discretionary spending above $10,000.
  • Bans purchase, lease, or custom outfitting of aircraft or vehicles for personal use of political appointees using federal funds.
  • Annual independent audit of all White House and executive office expenditures, published publicly within 30 days.
Violation: Treble repayment of concealed expenditure + misdemeanor for deliberate concealment
Why it works: "Custom luxury jets at your expense" is already a campaign ad. This law creates a permanent, searchable record — impossible to hide, impossible to spin, available to every journalist and every voter.
Pillar 3
VI. The Grift Tax Act
Punitive clawback on pay-to-play enrichment through public office
＋

Core Mechanism

  • 75% punitive tax on all income, assets, or benefits causally linked to government access or preferential treatment.
  • Applies to appointed officials, elected officials, contractors, lobbyists, and donors who receive measurable benefit from purchased access.
  • 10-year clawback window from the date of relevant appointment, contract award, or policy decision.
  • All proceeds fund the Public Accountability Fund — used exclusively for independent oversight infrastructure.
  • Dedicated Grift Tax Enforcement Division within IRS and DOJ, insulated from executive interference.
  • Whistleblowers receive 15% of recovered proceeds and full federal employment protection.
Penalty: 75% clawback + up to 10 years imprisonment for concealment
Why it works: "Grift Tax" is language voters already use. It names the crime plainly, generates revenue that funds oversight, and creates a deterrent that no existing law provides.
Pillar 3
VII. The Golden Rule Act
No public funds for personal branding or embellishment of federal property
＋
  • Prohibits federal funds from being used to rename, rebrand, or embellish any federal building, institution, park, program, or public space for partisan or personal purposes.
  • Congressional approval required for any White House or federal institution renovation exceeding $500,000 per fiscal year.
  • Mandates restoration of any federally protected space altered in violation of historic preservation standards — costs borne personally by the individual responsible.
  • All renaming decisions require a 90-day public comment period and approval by the relevant oversight body.
Violation: Immediate reversal order + personal liability for full restoration cost
Why it works: Gold-plating the People's House. Paving the Rose Garden. These images are viscerally offensive — and this law makes them personally expensive to the person who does them.
Pillar 1
VIII. The Emoluments Enforcement Act
Gives the Constitution's anti-corruption clauses real teeth
＋

The Constitution already prohibits presidents and federal officials from receiving financial benefits from foreign or domestic governments. These clauses have never been enforced with a standalone statute. This act provides penalties, standing, and mechanism.

  • Private right of action — any member of Congress or U.S. citizen may bring an emoluments violation claim in federal court, eliminating standing barriers.
  • All presidents, vice presidents, and cabinet members must place privately-held business assets in a blind trust within 90 days of taking office — no exceptions.
  • Mandatory quarterly public disclosure of all financial relationships between the sitting president's businesses and any government entity.
  • Dedicated Emoluments Oversight Panel within the GAO — subpoena power, independent budget.
Violation: Full disgorgement of emoluments received + up to $5M civil penalty per incident
Why it works: The Constitution already says this is illegal. This law simply makes the Constitution mean what it says — with a mechanism for enforcement that doesn't exist today.
Pillar 1
IX. The Appointment Integrity Act
Ends the donor-to-appointee pipeline. Restores merit to federal agencies.
＋
  • Senate confirmation required for all agency heads — no acting-indefinitely loopholes beyond 60 days.
  • 5-year ban on appointing anyone who donated more than $50,000 to the appointing president's campaign or affiliated PACs.
  • Mandatory public disclosure of all financial relationships between nominees and the appointing administration.
  • Written minimum qualifications verified by an independent panel of retired civil servants in the relevant field.
Violation: Appointment void + $500,000/month civil penalty per month of illegal tenure
Why it works: "No more buying your way into the cabinet" is a sentence everyone understands. The luxury-jet donor-appointee is the defining image of this era. This law makes it illegal — permanently.
Pillar 2
X. The Lobbying Cooling Off Act
Lifetime ban on federal lobbying for Congress. 10 years for all appointed officials.
＋
  • Lifetime ban on federal lobbying for all members of Congress and the President upon leaving office.
  • 10-year cooling off period for all Senate-confirmed presidential appointees.
  • Extends the lobbying definition to include strategic advisory and consulting arrangements that functionally constitute lobbying regardless of formal registration.
  • Public Post-Government Employment Registry — all former officials register employment for 10 years post-service.
Violation: Full fee disgorgement + 5-year federal prison + permanent bar from government work
Why it works: A lifetime lobbying ban for Congress polls above 70% with voters of both parties. "They go to Washington to work for us, then cash out to work against us" is universally understood.
